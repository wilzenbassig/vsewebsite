Green Building Materials

VSE works on a large variety of projects and we design efficiently and effectively with all applications of conventional materials. We fundamentally believe that green engineering is always good engineering and that good engineering can always be green engineering. There is an appropriate material use and strategy that is unique for every project's individual set of limiting variables.

Below we have compiled a list of materials we design with which, if used appropriately, can be particularly low carbon and support the most sustainable designs.

As carbon emissions have become the leading urgent environmental concern of sustainable building practices, VSE offers the service of quantifying the volume of embodied carbon the material choices in your structure could be responsible for. Embodied carbon refers to carbon dioxide emitted during the manufacture, transport and construction of building materials.

VSE strives to provide information guidance needed to design against the hazards of unsustainable construction as efficiently as we design against all other safety hazards documented in the building code.

According to the U.S. Environmental Protection Agency, buildings account for:

  • 38% of total U.S. carbon dioxide emissions (more than the transportation industry, which accounts for 33% of carbon dioxide emissions)
  • 72% of total U.S. electricity consumption, with residential buildings accounting for 53.7% of that total and commercial buildings accounting for the other 46.3%
  • 39% of total U.S. energy use
  • 14% of potable water consumption
  • 26% of waste output
  • 40% of raw materials use

Designing structures with green materials and practices in mind provides a great opportunity to make a significant, positive impact on the environment and the people who occupy the building. Diamond Piers

Diamond Piers are gravity bearing foundations that also allow for lateral support. These foundation systems require minimal soil excavation and no concrete pouring on site. The system uses a pre-cast concrete head with four pin rods that are drilled into the earth below the pre-cast head. The benefit of this system is reduced site disturbance and reduced concrete usage.


Rubble Trench

Rubble trenches are bearing foundation systems. A tamped aggregate base is used to create a trench that extends below the frost line. Above the aggregate a concrete grade beam with a small stem wall is used to distribute an even load to the tamped aggregate below. Using a rubble trench allows for a large reduction in the use of concrete and cement on a project. Additionally, they are great for water drainage but do not allow for lateral strength and are thus not recommended in some seismically active areas. Many of Frank Lloyd Wrights homes were designed with rubble trenches.

Geo Piers

Use of soil stabilization to mitigate the need for concrete pier systems - Can be non-cementitious pier systems similar to rammed earth technology.


Blue Concrete

This systems creates carbonate rock by recapturing CO2 from flue gas and other sources. This allows concrete aggregate to be used as a carbon sink while also reducing the amount of mined aggregate required on a project.


Fly Ash

Flyash is a by-product of burning coal. It is a cementitious materials that can be used as a binder in concrete. Using fly-ash in concrete can reduce the amount of cement that is required on a project.


Straw Bale

There are many difference ways to construct straw bale walls. They can be used for both gravity bearing and lateral support for a structure. Straw bale walls consist of stacked bales on the interior of the wall with a plaster finish on the interior and exterior. The plaster can be reinforced with wire mesh in order to provide lateral support.

Earthen Masonry Units

Earth Masonry units are made of rammed earth that is pressed into the same shape as a CMU block. The benefit of this system is two-fold; they do not require concrete and they are shaped into a form tha most contractors are familiar building with.


Optimum Value Engineering aka Advanced Framing

Most wood framed homes use framing at 16" o.c.. With advanced framing techniques, the spacing of joists is reconsidered for the specific loads on the project (usually allowing framing to be spaced at 24" o.c.). Further reduction in wood framing is achieved with a single top plate and attention to detail around corners, at window and point load framing.


Bamboo

Bamboo is a high strength material. Bamboo is very renewable.


Structural Insulated Panels (SIP)

Structural Insulated Panel (SIP) construction consists of an insulated foam core sandwiched between two structural facing systems.


Faswall/ICF

Insulated Concrete Forms (ICF) are concrete filled foam forms that can be used for gravity bearing and lateral support. These systems allow for a reduction in operational carbon.
